auto_stories Course creation & quality

Spark finds natural breakpoints in your teaching - when you transition topics, introduce new concepts, or pause for examples. It creates chapters with 3-8 minute micro-sections that students can actually finish. You can always adjust boundaries, rename chapters, or reorganize. But most creators find the first draft is already pretty solid - way better than trying to manually slice a 90-minute video.

Spark uses a "maker-checker" system: it generates notes and quizzes, then a separate verification step checks for accuracy and alignment with what you actually taught. If something looks off, it tries to fix it automatically. If it can't resolve an issue, it flags it for your review instead of shipping something wrong. The tutor only answers from your course content - not the open web - so it stays grounded in what you taught.
