payments Monetization & business

You set the price. Spark supports subscriptions (monthly/yearly), one-time payments, fixed-duration programs, and even "pay what you want" options. You can create different plans for different access levels (full course, just quizzes, live sessions, etc.). If you teach globally, you can set country-wise pricing so students see fair local prices automatically. It's flexible - price it how you want to run your business.

Spark uses Stripe (globally) or RazorPay (in India) for payments. Students can pay with cards, UPI, netbanking, wallets - whatever the payment provider supports in their country. International cards work too. You connect your account once (takes about 5 minutes), and payments flow directly to your bank. Transaction fees are low, and you can choose to pass them on to students or absorb them.

Yes! Every student gets a unified portal where they can access courses, join live sessions, watch recordings, take quizzes, and chat with the tutor - all in one place. They see only what their subscription unlocks. It's clean, mobile-friendly, and feels like a real learning platform. No juggling multiple links or platforms.

Absolutely. You can create discount codes with expiry dates and usage limits. You can also set up student segments (like "new this month" or "lapsed subscribers") and restrict codes to specific groups. There's a wallet system where students can earn credits, and referral programs are on the roadmap. All the standard promotion tools you'd expect.

If you teach globally, you can set different prices for different countries on the same plan. Students automatically see the right price based on their location at checkout. This helps you make your course accessible worldwide without underpricing your core market. No manual payment links or tracking exceptions - it just works.

Subscriptions move automatically through states: Upcoming → Active → Expiring → Expired. Students get renewal reminders, access is controlled automatically, and you can see who's active, who's about to expire, and who's lapsed. Students can hold multiple subscriptions (like course + live sessions). If life happens, you can pause or extend subscriptions without breaking access. It's all automated - no manual tracking.

school Student experience

Students get a single link to your student portal. They sign in with Google (or email), and they see everything they have access to - courses, live sessions, recordings - in one place. No separate logins for different features. It's clean, organized, and mobile-friendly. They can bookmark it and come back anytime.

Yes, the student portal is fully responsive and works great on phones and tablets. Students can watch videos, read notes, take quizzes, and chat with the tutor - all from their mobile device. The interface adapts to smaller screens, so nothing feels cramped or hard to use. Perfect for learning on the go.

The tutor answers questions about your course content - 24/7. It stays grounded in what you actually taught, not the open web. Every answer shows which chapters or notes it came from, so students know the source. If a question is outside your course scope, the tutor says so instead of making something up. It's like having a teaching assistant that never sleeps, but only knows what you taught.

Yes. Students see their progress through each course - which chapters they've completed, quiz scores, time spent learning. It's all visible in their portal. For you, there are detailed reports showing which students are struggling with which concepts, so you know where to focus your teaching time. The reporting is built to answer "What should I do with my next hour?" not just show vanity metrics.

Your 90-minute videos get broken into 3-8 minute sections. Students can finish a section, take a quick quiz, and feel like they made progress - even in a busy day. Instead of "I'll watch that whole video later" (which never happens), they can squeeze in 10 minutes here and there. More small wins = more engagement = better learning. It's how people actually learn today, not how we wish they would.

folder Content management

Spark hosts your videos securely in the cloud. They're encrypted, protected from downloads and sharing, and streamed with adaptive quality (so they load fast on any connection). You don't need a separate video platform - everything lives in Spark. If you want, videos can also auto-upload to Google Drive as a backup.

Spark is designed to handle large catalogs - hundreds of videos, thousands of hours. There aren't hard limits that would stop you from building a serious course library. If you're uploading massive amounts, we'll work with you on storage, but for most creators, it's not something you'll hit.

Yes! You can create as many courses as you want. Each one is independent - its own structure, students, pricing, and content. Students can enroll in multiple courses, and you can bundle them or sell them separately. Build your course library over time, one video at a time.

Course editing features are coming soon. Right now, you can add new videos to existing courses, and they'll get processed the same way. Full editing controls - updating chapters, editing notes, removing sections - are on the roadmap. For now, if you need to make changes, you can regenerate sections or work with our team. We're building this out because we know courses need to evolve over time.

Yes. Your recordings and all generated content (chapters, notes, quizzes, course structure) are yours. Spark is being built to support exporting to standard formats like SCORM, Common Cartridge, or direct integration with Moodle, Canvas, and other LMS platforms. You can also download structured data (JSON, HTML) if you need it. You're never locked in.
