Spark isn't just an "AI summarizer." It's a comprehensive production pipeline that understands pedagogy, structure, and student needs.

Stage 1

Ingest & Understand

Spark takes your raw video and performs a word-level transcription. It then uses LLMs to analyze the narrative flow, finding natural "chapter" boundaries based on topic shifts, not just pauses.

  • ✓ Audio optimization & dead air detection
  • ✓ Topic-based boundary detection
  • ✓ Multi-turn validation loops
[Screenshot: Raw Transcript vs Structured Chapters]
Stage 2

Polish & Refine

Raw lectures are messy. Spark cleans them up. It identifies chapters that are too long ("clumped") and intelligently splits them. It trims silence and polishes metadata so every video feels like a purposeful lesson.

[Visual: Timeline Splitting & Polishing]
Stage 3

Generate Course Assets

This is where the magic happens. Spark generates comprehensive study notes for every chapter. It uses Computer Vision to extract key slides from your video feed, de-duplicating similar frames to create a clean slide deck.

[Screenshot: Generated Notes & Slide Grid]
Stage 4

The AI Tutor

Spark indexes everything. When a student asks a question, the AI Tutor answers only using your content (RAG), citing specific chapters and slides. It solves the "hallucination" problem by being strictly grounded in your expertise.
