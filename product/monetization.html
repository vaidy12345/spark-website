Monetization

The same system that makes your course also runs your business.

Spark doesn’t stop at content. It comes with the rails to package everything you do—courses, live classes, workshops, and recordings—into clear offerings that students can buy and stick with.

1. What you can sell

Once Spark has turned your recordings into structured courses, you can decide how to offer them. The same account can handle a mix of:

  • Courses – Spark‑generated courses sold as standalone products or bundled into larger programs.
  • Live classes – recurring group classes, one‑on‑ones, and workshops (online or offline).
  • Recordings & uploads – auto‑recorded live sessions and existing video libraries offered on‑demand.

For your students, it all looks like a single “virtual studio”: one place where they discover what you offer, sign up, join sessions, and come back to your courses when they have time.

2. Plans and pricing that match how you really teach

Spark’s pricing engine is built for teachers who don’t fit into one neat box. You can run:

  • Paid, donation‑based, and free plans – experiment with different entry points and “pay what you want” tiers.
  • Online, offline, and hybrid plans – connect a single plan to in‑person classes, online sessions, or both.
  • Private plans – plans visible only to specific email addresses or student segments.
  • Tiered and bundled pricing – ladders based on class counts or duration, plus upsells and cross‑sells between plans.

3. Payments and subscriptions that run on their own

Spark is designed to collect and track payments with as little manual work as possible:

  • Modern online payment methods, including cards and local options, with support for multiple currencies.
  • Automatic subscription lifecycles: upcoming, active, expiring, and expired, with reminders sent at the right time.
  • Support for students holding multiple plans at once—for example, a regular class plan plus a short exam bootcamp.
  • Options to freeze or extend subscriptions when life happens, without losing track of entitlements.

4. Virtual studio and secure hosting

Students get a single “home” with your brand on it:

  • Virtual studio portal – shows only the classes and courses a student can actually attend, with one‑click joins for live sessions.
  • Time‑zone aware schedules – no more messages asking “what time is this in my country?”.
  • Secure video hosting – streaming tuned for real‑world internet speeds, with safeguards against casual downloading and sharing.

5. Promotions and engagement that don’t feel spammy

Spark includes tools to nudge students without turning your teaching into a discount circus:

  • Discount codes for thoughtful campaigns instead of permanent sales.
  • Wallet‑style credits and cashbacks for rewarding loyal students.
  • Referral and gifting mechanics that let your happiest students bring others in.
  • Consistency‑based rewards (leaderboards, badges) to celebrate students who keep showing up.
