From “raw lecture” to “course with rails” in a few clicks

Spark is opinionated about what a serious learning experience should look like. It doesn’t just clip your video and walk away—it builds a full stack around it: chapters and micro-sections, study notes that respect your voice, chapter-level quizzes, and a per-course AI tutor that answers questions only from what you’ve actually covered.

Under the hood there are many moving pieces, but the outer experience for you is deliberately simple: upload or connect your recordings, choose where you want Spark to focus, and then review what it produced before you invite students in.

On the student side, everything shows up in one modern interface: a three-pane player with video, notes, quizzes, and a tutor that sits next to your content instead of replacing it.
