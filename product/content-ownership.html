Content ownership

Your content stays yours. Full stop.

Spark ingests your recordings and generates chapters, notes, slides, quizzes, and tutor answers from them. None of that changes the basic rule: it’s still your intellectual property.

What you keep

  • Your original recordings – every video or audio file you upload or connect.
  • All derived learning material – chapters, sectioned clips, study notes, slides, quizzes, and tutor responses produced from your content.
  • The structured “map” of your courses – outlines, chapter structures, and other metadata that describe how your teaching is organized.

What Spark does with it

  • Hosts and serves your content to the students you authorize.
  • Uses it to power features you explicitly turn on (courses, quizzes, tutor, search across your own material).
  • Stores enough data to keep things running smoothly and to help you improve the learning experience over time.

Spark does not repackage your content as a generic library, sell it to other teachers, or blend it into a shared pool that others can draw from.

Portability and future‑proofing

As your needs evolve, you should be able to carry your work with you. Spark is being designed so that structured representations of your courses and assessments can be exported and reused elsewhere where appropriate.

Put simply: Spark is the engine and the rails; the content and the value of that content are yours.
